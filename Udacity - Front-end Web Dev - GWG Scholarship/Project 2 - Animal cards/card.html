Fennec Fox

Fennecs are one of only two foxes allowed as a pet in the United States, along with the Russian domesticated red fox.

  • Scientific Name: Vulpes zerda, Fennecus zerda (previous, debated)
  • Average Height: 20.3cm
  • Average Lifespan: 14 years (in captivity)
  • Habitat: Deserts

A fennec fox is a small nocturnal fox native to the Sahara, Arabian desert, and Sinai Peninsula. They are the smallest member of both the genus Vulpes and the broader family Canidae, though their status in Vulpes is a matter of debate. They are omnivorous, feed on insects, birds, and small mammals, and they are hunted by eagle owls, jackals, and striped hyenas. They mate for life and travel in packs.
